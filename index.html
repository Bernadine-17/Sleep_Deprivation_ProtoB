Background

Sleep deprivation is known to negatively affect the mind in various ways. Saccadeds are the semi-involuntary, fast movements of your eyes to focus on a new point. Although they can be controlled consciously, their involuntary element allows them to be a measurement tool for sleep deprivation. When sleep deprived, the velocity of saccadic movements (degrees/sec) are slowed.

Instructions

This app cannot measure your saccadic velocity but will measure a variation of this by recording your response time using horizontal eye movements to determine if you are sleep deprived or not. Hold your device horizontally (longways) around an arms length distance from your face and refresh the page so that the testing canvas appears in the correct orientation. When ready, press start and stare at the red dot. A black letter will flash on either side of the dot. When you see this letter, press the stop button. This will record an individual response time in seconds. You will be alerted when you complete 20 trials. Find the test under the "Test" tab above or scroll down.
